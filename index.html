📐 What is SVD?

For any real matrix \( A \) of size \( m \times n \), the Singular Value Decomposition is:

\[ A = U \Sigma V^T \]
  • U (\(m \times m\)): Left singular vectors (orthonormal columns).
  • \(\Sigma\) (\(m \times n\)): Diagonal matrix with singular values \(\sigma_1 \ge \sigma_2 \ge ... \ge 0\).
  • V (\(n \times n\)): Right singular vectors (orthonormal columns).

🌀 Geometric Meaning of SVD Components

Any linear transformation \( \mathbf{x} \mapsto A\mathbf{x} \) can be decomposed as:

Rotation/Reflection (by \(V^T\)) → Scaling (by \(\Sigma\)) → Rotation (by \(U\)).

The singular values \(\sigma_i\) represent the lengths of the semi-axes of the ellipse (ellipsoid) that is the image of the unit sphere under \(A\).

🔵 Unit circle → 🟠 Transformed ellipse

\(A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)
\(\sigma_1 \approx 2.618, \sigma_2 \approx 0.382\)

🎯 Right singular vectors v₁, v₂ (blue) & Transformed A·v₁, A·v₂ (orange)
💡 Key Insight: Largest singular value = maximum gain. Columns of V are orthonormal basis that align with stretching axes.

📝 Step-by-Step Hand Calculations

🔍 How to Calculate Eigenvalues of AᵀA and Singular Vectors

General Procedure: (1) Compute AᵀA. (2) Solve det(AᵀA - λI)=0 → eigenvalues λᵢ. (3) σᵢ = √λᵢ. (4) Find eigenvectors of AᵀA → right singular vectors vᵢ. (5) Left vectors: uᵢ = (1/σᵢ) A vᵢ.

📐 Example for A = \(\begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\): AᵀA = \(\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 3 \\ 3 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\). Characteristic: λ² -7λ +1=0 → λ≈6.854,0.146 → σ≈2.618,0.382. v₁≈(0.8507,0.5257), v₂≈(-0.5257,0.8507). u₁ = A v₁/σ₁ ≈ (0.8507,0.5257), u₂ = A v₂/σ₂ ≈ (-0.5257,0.8507).

🔷 Example 1: 2×2 Matrix

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

1️⃣ AᵀA = \[ \begin{bmatrix} 9 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]
2️⃣ Eigenvalues: λ₁=9, λ₂=1 → σ₁=3, σ₂=1
3️⃣ Right vectors V: v₁=(1,0)ᵀ, v₂=(0,1)ᵀ → V=I
4️⃣ Left vectors U: u₁=(1,0)ᵀ, u₂=(0,1)ᵀ → U=I
✅ A = U Σ Vᵀ

🔷 Example 2: 2×3 Matrix (wide)

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

1️⃣ AᵀA (3×3): \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \]
2️⃣ Eigenvalues: λ₁=3, λ₂=1, λ₃=0 → σ₁=√3≈1.732, σ₂=1, σ₃=0
3️⃣ Right vectors V: v₁=(1,1,2)/√6, v₂=(1,-1,0)/√2
4️⃣ Left vectors U: u₁ = A v₁/σ₁ = (2,2)/√6, u₂ = A v₂ = (1,-1)/√2
✅ Σ = \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1.732 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]

🔷 Example 3: 3×2 Matrix (tall)

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

1️⃣ AᵀA (2×2): \[ \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \]
2️⃣ Eigenvalues: λ₁=3, λ₂=1 → σ₁=√3≈1.732, σ₂=1
3️⃣ Right vectors V: v₁=(1,1)/√2, v₂=(-1,1)/√2
4️⃣ Left vectors U: u₁ = A v₁/σ₁ = (1,1,2)/√6, u₂ = A v₂ = (-1,1,0)/√2
✅ Σ = \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1.732 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]

🚀 SVD in Machine Learning & Large Language Models

🧠 1. Dimensionality Reduction (PCA): SVD on centered data gives principal components.
🤖 2. LLM Weight Compression & LoRA: Low-rank adaptation uses SVD insight: W = W₀ + BA with r ≪ d.
🔍 3. Recommendation Systems: User-item matrix factorization via SVD.
📚 4. Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA): SVD on term-document matrices reveals topics.
⚡ 5. Attention Approximation: Linformer uses low-rank attention via SVD.
📉 6. Model Pruning: Factorize dense layers into two smaller layers.

💡 Concrete LLM Example: LoRA

In fine-tuning LLaMA-7B, LoRA injects trainable low-rank matrices: h = W₀x + (α/r) B A x. This leverages the intrinsic low-rank property of weight updates — a direct SVD insight, reducing memory from 140GB to 14GB.
